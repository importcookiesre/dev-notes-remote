

Both containers and Virtual Machines (VMs) are tools used to isolate applications and their dependencies, they achieve this isolation at entirely different layers of the technology stack.

### The Real Estate Analogy

- **Virtual Machine (The Freestanding House):** You buy a plot of land (the physical server) and build a complete house on it. It has its own foundation, its own plumbing, and its own electrical grid. It is completely isolated, but building and maintaining that separate infrastructure for every single house takes massive amounts of resources and time.
    
- **Container (The Apartment Building):** You build a high-rise. Every tenant (application) gets their own secure apartment with a locked door. However, they all share the building's underlying plumbing, electricity, and foundation (the Host OS Kernel). It is highly efficient, allowing you to pack many more tenants into the same physical footprint.
